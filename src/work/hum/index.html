We narrowed down the scope of what would be possible in the first version of Hum. We focused on capturing audio, lyrics, and sorting.

Since sorting was necessary, we needed the ability to add meta data to each song. We landed on being able to specify key, tuning, and mood. Liner notes

As we continued to build and refine Hum, I wanted to take the opportunity to feature artists and their songwriting process. This started as a series of videos that Joseph and I would produce. We started small with local Minneapolis artist we knew and loved. The idea was simple: Visit an artist in their element, this could be an apartment, a practice space, working at a mall kiosk‚Äîanything, really, and film them at their craft.

Like the Anthem, our process starts with the score first. We interview the artist, cut the interview together, and then build a score underneath their spoken bits. Then, we visit their space and capture as much as possible, cutting to the score.
